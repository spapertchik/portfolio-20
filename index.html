N.C.C. Responsive Web Redesign

Context:

Nature Conservancy Canada is a NPO. We were asked to redesign an existing website for the organization to improve the UI and eliminate difficulties users were experiencing while browsing the site.

Project Brief:

Our fundamental task was to evaluate the website and improve upon it in such a way that was helpful to the organization and users. It was up to us to define what those improvements needed to be.

My Role:

Research, Data synthesis, Information architecture, Wireframing, Layout design, Mock-up building.

Research

First things first!

All our design decisions were based on our research. In order to define areas that needed improvement we conducted a series of user tests and interviews, as well as heuristic evaluation and competitor analysis.

Interviews

Interviews were the first step we took to gain valuable insights on how the website was currently being used and where users were finding the most pain points. Our main objectives were to gain insight on how people preffer to contribute to non-profit organizations, what they felt was most important when looking for ways to get involved and how they accomplished such tasks. A user test was also set up with each interviewee so that we may observe how users interacted with the website. This was done for desktop and mobile views.

What We Learned:
  • Volunteering was just as important as donating.
  • Users are more trusting of websites with current user interface style and pattern.
  • Many users had difficulty fiinding what they were looking for while on the site.
